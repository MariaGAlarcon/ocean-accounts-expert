# Data Sources for Social and Governance Accounts

This reference lists international and national data sources relevant to compiling ocean social and governance accounts. Use this alongside the social data audit (stage 2 of the Social Account Development process) to identify what data is available for your country.

---

## Census and Demographic Data

| Source | Description | URL | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| National census | Population by age, gender, ethnicity, education, location | National statistics office | Most countries conduct a census every 10 years. Check whether coastal areas can be identified. |
| DHS (Demographic and Health Surveys) | Health, nutrition, population data for developing countries | dhsprogram.com | Available for 90+ countries. Includes nutrition, child health, and household data. |
| MICS (Multiple Indicator Cluster Surveys) | Household surveys on children and women | mics.unicef.org | Covers health, education, child protection. Available for 100+ countries. |
| WorldPop | Gridded population estimates | worldpop.org | High-resolution spatial population data. Can be overlaid with coastline to estimate coastal population. |
| UN Population Division | National and subnational population estimates | population.un.org | Useful for countries without recent census data. |

## Livelihoods and Employment

| Source | Description | URL | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| ILO labor force surveys | Employment data by sector, gender, age | ilostat.ilo.org | Check for ISIC codes related to fishing, aquaculture, maritime transport, and coastal tourism. |
| National fisheries employment records | Employment in capture fisheries and aquaculture | National fisheries ministry | Coverage varies widely. Small-scale and informal fisheries are often undercounted. |
| FAO small-scale fisheries data | Guidelines and data on small-scale fisheries | fao.org/fishery | FAO Voluntary Guidelines for Securing Sustainable Small-Scale Fisheries provide a framework. |
| National tourism employment data | Employment in tourism by region | National tourism board | Check whether coastal/marine tourism can be disaggregated from total tourism. |
| UNWTO tourism statistics | International tourism data by country | unwto.org | National-level data. Does not disaggregate coastal tourism. |
| National household surveys | Household income, expenditure, livelihoods | National statistics office | Often the best source for livelihood data at subnational level. |

## Time-Use Data

| Source | Description | URL | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| UN time-use surveys database | Database of national time-use surveys | unstats.un.org/unsd/gender/timeuse | Lists which countries have conducted time-use surveys and their methodology. |
| National time-use surveys | How people spend their time by activity category | National statistics office | Where they exist, these are the primary source for flow data (hours). Check activity classifications for ocean-related categories. |

**Major gap:** Very few countries have ocean-specific time-use data. Standard time-use classifications (ICATUS) do not disaggregate ocean-related activities from general categories. For example, "fishing for household consumption" may be grouped with "food preparation" rather than identified separately. Primary data collection using adapted time-use instruments will likely be needed for flow indicators.

## Wellbeing

| Source | Description | URL | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| WHO health statistics | Health indicators by country | who.int/data | Life expectancy, disease burden, nutrition. National-level data. |
| World Happiness Report / Gallup World Poll | Life satisfaction, subjective wellbeing | worldhappiness.report | National-level subjective wellbeing data. Cannot be disaggregated to coastal areas without primary data collection. |
| National wellbeing surveys | Country-specific wellbeing measures | National statistics office | Some countries have dedicated wellbeing frameworks (e.g., UK ONS, NZ Living Standards Framework, Bhutan GNH). Check whether your country has one. |
| Global Burden of Disease | Health burden by cause and location | healthdata.org | Can identify health issues relevant to coastal populations (e.g., waterborne diseases, nutrition deficiencies). |

## Equity and Access

| Source | Description | URL | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| World Bank poverty data | Poverty headcount, Gini coefficient, income distribution | data.worldbank.org | National and sometimes subnational poverty data. Check for coastal disaggregation. |
| National household income surveys | Income distribution, expenditure patterns | National statistics office | The best source for distributional analysis at subnational level. |
| Gini coefficient data | Income inequality measure | World Bank, national statistics | Available for most countries at national level. |
| Resource access and tenure data | Land rights, sea rights, fishing access | Land ministry, fisheries ministry | Coverage varies greatly. Informal and customary rights are often undocumented. |
| Fishing permit and license data | Who has formal access to fisheries | Fisheries ministry | Can reveal who is included/excluded from formal access. Check for gender and income disaggregation. |

## Cultural Connections

| Source | Description | URL | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| UNESCO cultural heritage databases | World Heritage Sites, Intangible Cultural Heritage | whc.unesco.org, ich.unesco.org | Lists formally recognized cultural heritage. Marine and coastal sites can be identified. |
| National cultural surveys | Cultural participation, practices, heritage | Cultural affairs ministry | Not all countries have these. Where they exist, check for ocean-related categories. |
| Traditional knowledge databases | Documented traditional ecological knowledge | Various (national, academic) | Where they exist, these are valuable but may have access restrictions to protect community rights. |
| Community-held records | Oral histories, ceremony records, cultural maps | Community organizations, elders | Much cultural data is qualitative and held by communities, not in formal databases. Primary data collection through respectful engagement is essential. |

**Important note:** Cultural data is the area where formal databases are least likely to capture what matters. Community engagement (stages 4-6 of the social account development process) is essential for understanding cultural connections to the ocean. Qualitative methods (interviews, focus groups, participatory mapping) are often more appropriate than quantitative surveys.

## Governance

| Source | Description | URL | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Protected Planet | Global database of protected areas including MPAs | protectedplanet.net | MPA boundaries, designation type, management category, governance type. Spatial data available. |
| National MPA management records | Management plans, monitoring reports, compliance data | Environment ministry, MPA authority | Quality and completeness vary. Check for monitoring and evaluation records. |
| Fisheries governance records | Regulations, co-management agreements, compliance data | Fisheries ministry | Includes formal regulations, community-based management agreements, enforcement records. |
| OECD governance indicators | Government effectiveness, regulatory quality | oecd.org | Available for OECD and partner countries. National-level indicators. |
| World Governance Indicators | Rule of law, government effectiveness, voice and accountability | info.worldbank.org/governance/wgi | National-level governance indicators for 200+ countries. Not ocean-specific but provides context. |
| National gazette / legal databases | Laws, regulations, and policies related to ocean governance | Government legal records | Primary source for mapping the regulatory framework. |
| Fisheries Management Organizations | Regional agreements, quotas, compliance | RFMOs (e.g., WCPFC, IOTC, CCAMLR) | For governance of shared or high-seas fisheries resources. |

## Spatial Data for Social Accounts

| Source | Description | URL | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| WorldPop + coastline overlay | Coastal population density | worldpop.org | Combine gridded population with a coastal buffer (e.g., 10 km, 50 km) to estimate coastal population. |
| Census data mapped to BSUs | Population and social data at fine spatial resolution | National statistics office | Where census data includes geographic identifiers, it can be mapped to Basic Spatial Units used in ocean accounts. |
| OpenStreetMap | Community infrastructure, roads, settlements | openstreetmap.org | Can help identify coastal settlements and infrastructure. |
| National spatial data infrastructure | Georeferenced administrative boundaries, land use | National mapping agency | Check for coastal zone delineation. |

## Tips for Data Access

1. **Start with the national statistics office.** They hold or know about most social data in the country.

2. **Check what is publicly available online** before making formal requests. Many datasets are downloadable from statistics office websites.

3. **International survey programs** (DHS, MICS, ILO) often have microdata available for researchers. Check their data access policies.

4. **Fisheries data is often fragmented.** Employment data may be in the statistics office, catch data in the fisheries ministry, and livelihood data in household surveys. You may need to combine multiple sources.

5. **Cultural and governance data requires relationship building.** Government records may be incomplete or difficult to access. Community-held knowledge requires respectful engagement and may not be appropriate to include in formal databases without community consent.

6. **Spatial resolution is a common challenge.** National-level data may not be disaggregated to coastal areas. Where possible, use surveys with geographic identifiers that allow coastal disaggregation.

7. **Data quality notes are essential.** For every data source you use, record its limitations, coverage gaps, and quality issues. This metadata is part of the social account.
