# Social Account Indicator Reference Tables

These tables provide a reference list of indicators for social accounts, organized by the stocks and flows framework from the GOAP Technical Guidance Circular. These are illustrative -- countries should select and adapt indicators based on their policy needs and data availability through the co-design process (stage 1).

---

## Notes on Using These Tables

1. **These are illustrative, not prescriptive.** Select indicators based on your policy question and data availability. A pilot social account may use only 5-10 indicators.

2. **Qualitative data matters.** Many social dimensions cannot be captured by numbers alone. Narrative descriptions of cultural connections, community perspectives on equity, and governance process quality should accompany quantitative indicators.

3. **Flow data in hours is a major gap.** Very few countries have ocean-specific time-use data. Where time-use surveys exist, they rarely disaggregate ocean-related activities. Primary data collection (stage 5 of the social account development process) will often be needed for flow indicators.

4. **Stock indicators are more readily available.** Census data, health statistics, education records, and governance data are collected in most countries. The social data audit (stage 2) will identify what is accessible.

5. **Spatial resolution matters.** National-level data may mask important local variation. Where possible, use data that can be mapped to coastal areas or BSUs.

6. **Disaggregation is essential for equity analysis.** Indicators should be disaggregated by gender, age, ethnicity, income group, and geography where data allows. This is what makes social accounts useful for equity and access analysis.
