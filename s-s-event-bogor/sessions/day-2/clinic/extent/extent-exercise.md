# Extent Account Exercise

**Time:** 20-30 minutes
**What you need:** Calculator or Excel, this sheet

---

## Scenario

You are building an extent account for a coastal district with three marine ecosystem types. Field surveys and satellite imagery give you the following data.

### Opening period (2018)

| Ecosystem type | Area (ha) |
|---------------|----------:|
| Coral reefs | 1,240 |
| Seagrass meadows | 860 |
| Mangroves | 315 |
| Sand/rubble/deep water | 7,585 |
| **Total accounting area** | **10,000** |

### Closing period (2023)

| Ecosystem type | Area (ha) |
|---------------|----------:|
| Coral reefs | 1,180 |
| Seagrass meadows | 825 |
| Mangroves | 340 |
| Sand/rubble/deep water | 7,655 |
| **Total accounting area** | **10,000** |

### Change matrix (from satellite classification comparison)

| Opening \ Closing | Coral | Seagrass | Mangrove | Other |
|-------------------|------:|---------:|---------:|------:|
| Coral | 1,140 | 15 | 0 | 85 |
| Seagrass | 10 | 790 | 5 | 55 |
| Mangrove | 0 | 0 | 300 | 15 |
| Other | 30 | 20 | 35 | 7,500 |

---

## Part 3: Interpret the transitions

Answer these questions:

1. Which ecosystem type gained the most area? Where did the new area come from?

2. Which ecosystem type lost the most area? What did it convert to?

3. The change matrix shows 85 ha of coral became "Other" (sand/rubble). What might explain this ecologically?

4. Mangroves gained 35 ha from "Other." What does this likely represent?

5. Is the total accounting area the same for opening and closing? Why must this be true?

## Answer Key

### Part 1

| | Coral | Seagrass | Mangroves | Other | Total |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Opening | 1,240 | 860 | 315 | 7,585 | 10,000 |
| Additions | 40 | 35 | 40 | 155 | 270 |
| Reductions | 100 | 70 | 15 | 85 | 270 |
| Net change | -60 | -35 | +25 | +70 | 0 |
| Closing | 1,180 | 825 | 340 | 7,655 | 10,000 |

Additions for coral: 10 (from seagrass) + 0 (from mangrove) + 30 (from other) = 40
Reductions for coral: 15 (to seagrass) + 0 (to mangrove) + 85 (to other) = 100

### Part 2

| Ecosystem | Opening | Closing | Net change | Change (%) |
|-----------|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Coral | 1,240 | 1,180 | -60 | -4.8% |
| Seagrass | 860 | 825 | -35 | -4.1% |
| Mangroves | 315 | 340 | +25 | +7.9% |

### Part 3

1. Mangroves gained the most proportionally (+7.9%). The 35 ha came from "Other," likely representing natural expansion or restoration of mangroves into previously unvegetated coastal areas.
2. Coral lost the most area (-60 ha). Most of it (85 ha) converted to "Other" (sand/rubble), offset partially by 30 ha gained from "Other."
3. Coral becoming sand/rubble likely represents reef degradation: dead coral framework breaking down into rubble after bleaching events, storm damage, or chronic stress.
4. Mangrove gain from "Other" likely represents either natural colonization of new areas (e.g., sediment accumulation creating suitable substrate) or mangrove restoration/replanting programs.
5. Yes, the total must be the same (10,000 ha). The accounting area boundary does not change, so every hectare classified in the opening period must still be classified in the closing period. Area is conserved; it can change category but not appear or disappear.
