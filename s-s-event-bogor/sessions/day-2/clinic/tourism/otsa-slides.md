# Ocean Tourism Satellite Account -- Clinic Walkthrough Slides

---

## Slide 1: What is an OTSA?

An **Ocean Tourism Satellite Account (OTSA)** is a focused satellite account that measures the economic contribution of ocean and coastal tourism.

- It is a **subset of the OESA** (Ocean Economy Satellite Account), drilling deeper into the tourism sector
- Aligned with the **TSA (Tourism Satellite Account)** framework developed by UNWTO
- Provides a structured way to isolate the coastal/marine portion of a country's tourism economy
- Answers the question: **How much does ocean tourism contribute to our economy?**

---

## Slide 2: What Does an OTSA Measure?

The OTSA captures four core indicators for coastal and marine tourism:

| Indicator | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Coastal/marine visitor arrivals** | Number of tourists whose primary purpose is ocean-related |
| **Tourism expenditure** | Spending by coastal/marine tourists on accommodation, food, transport, recreation |
| **Tourism direct GVA** | Gross value added generated by coastal tourism industries |
| **Employment in coastal tourism sectors** | Jobs in accommodation, food & beverage, recreation, and transport serving coastal tourists |

Key sectors covered:
- Accommodation (hotels, resorts, guesthouses in coastal areas)
- Food and beverage services in coastal zones
- Marine recreation (diving, snorkeling, boat tours, whale watching)
- Transport to and within coastal destinations

---

## Slide 3: Three-Step Approach

### Step 1: Define the Coastal Tourism Boundary
- Which activities count as "coastal/marine tourism"?
- Define geographic scope (coastal municipalities, distance from shore)
- Define activity scope (beach, diving, fishing charters, marine parks)

### Step 2: Estimate the Tourism Partial
- What share of national tourism is coastal or marine?
- Use visitor surveys, geographic proxies, or activity-based methods
- This is the most critical and data-intensive step

### Step 3: Compile Tourism Indicators
- Apply the tourism partial to national tourism data
- Calculate coastal arrivals, expenditure, GVA, and employment
- Present results in a standardised OTSA table

---

## Slide 4: Data Sources

| Source | What It Provides |
|--------|-----------------|
| **National tourism authority/board** | Arrivals data, accommodation statistics, tourism employment |
| **UNWTO** | International tourism statistics, benchmarks, TSA guidance |
| **Accommodation statistics** | Occupancy rates, bed-nights in coastal vs. inland areas |
| **Visitor surveys** | Motivation data ("Did you come for the beach/reef/marine activities?") |
| **Existing TSA** | If available, the national Tourism Satellite Account provides GVA and employment breakdowns |

Key survey question for establishing the tourism partial:
> "What was your primary motivation for visiting? (a) Beach/coastal activities, (b) Marine wildlife/diving/snorkeling, (c) Other..."

---

## Slide 5: Examples

### South Africa
- Coastal tourism accounts for **19.6% of total tourism expenditure**
- Based on beach activity participation rates from the national visitor survey
- Demonstrates the visitor-motivation method for estimating the tourism partial

### Belize
- Tourism is the **largest ocean economy sector**
- Marine tourism (reef diving, snorkeling, fishing) is the primary draw for international visitors
- The barrier reef system is the foundation of the tourism economy

### Key Insight
Even in countries not traditionally seen as "ocean economies," coastal tourism often turns out to be a significant contributor once properly measured.

## Slide 6: How OTSA Connects to Other Accounts

```
                    National Accounts
                          |
              +-----------+-----------+
              |                       |
         OESA (Ocean Economy     SEEA-EA (Ecosystem
         Satellite Account)       Accounting)
              |                       |
         OTSA (Tourism sector    Recreation &
         of the ocean economy)   cultural services
              |                       |
              +-----------+-----------+
                          |
              Comprehensive picture of
              ocean tourism value
```

- **OTSA feeds into the OESA** as the tourism sector of the ocean economy
- **Cultural ecosystem services from SEEA-EA** (recreation value of reefs, beaches, marine parks) complement the OTSA from the environmental side
- Together they show the full picture:
  - **OTSA** = what tourists spend (demand-side, economic)
  - **SEEA recreation** = what the ecosystem provides (supply-side, environmental)
- This integration helps policymakers understand both the economic returns and the natural capital that underpins them
