# Ocean Tourism Satellite Account Exercise

## Scenario: Coastal Country with a Significant Tourism Sector

You are compiling an OTSA for a coastal country. The following national-level data are available.

---

## Given Data

| Item | Value |
|------|-------|
| Total international arrivals | 500,000 per year |
| Total domestic tourism trips | 2,000,000 per year |
| Total tourism expenditure | USD 800 million per year |
| Tourism direct GVA | USD 320 million per year |
| Tourism employment | 45,000 jobs |
| National GDP | USD 5,000 million |

### From a visitor survey:
- **35%** of international visitors report coastal/marine activities as their primary motivation
- **20%** of domestic visitors report coastal/marine activities as their primary motivation
- International visitors spend **2.5x more per trip** than domestic visitors

---

## Part 1: Calculate Coastal Arrivals

Calculate the number of coastal/marine-motivated arrivals for each group.

- International coastal arrivals = ?
- Domestic coastal trips = ?
- Total coastal arrivals = ?

---

## Part 2: Calculate the Weighted Tourism Partial for Expenditure

Since international visitors spend 2.5x more per trip than domestic visitors, you need a weighted partial.

Let domestic spending per trip = 1 unit. Then international spending per trip = 2.5 units.

- Total expenditure weight for international = 500,000 x 2.5 = ?
- Total expenditure weight for domestic = 2,000,000 x 1 = ?
- Coastal-weighted expenditure (international) = ?
- Coastal-weighted expenditure (domestic) = ?
- Weighted tourism partial = ?

---

## Part 3: Calculate Coastal Tourism Expenditure

- Coastal tourism expenditure = Total tourism expenditure x Weighted tourism partial = ?

---

## Part 4: Calculate Coastal Tourism GVA

Assume the GVA-to-expenditure ratio is the same for coastal tourism as for total tourism.

- GVA-to-expenditure ratio = Tourism direct GVA / Total tourism expenditure = ?
- Coastal tourism GVA = Coastal tourism expenditure x GVA-to-expenditure ratio = ?

---

## Part 5: Calculate Coastal Tourism Employment

Use a simple weighted partial for employment (same approach as expenditure, but using arrivals rather than spending weights, since employment relates more to visitor numbers).

- Coastal arrivals partial = Total coastal arrivals / Total arrivals = ?
- Coastal tourism employment = Total tourism employment x Coastal arrivals partial = ?

---

## Part 6: Build the OTSA Summary Table

Fill in the table:

| Indicator | Total Tourism | Tourism Partial | Coastal/Ocean Tourism |
|-----------|--------------|----------------|-----------------------|
| Arrivals (number) | 2,500,000 | | |
| Expenditure (USD M) | 800 | | |
| Direct GVA (USD M) | 320 | | |
| Employment (jobs) | 45,000 | | |
| % of national GDP | 6.4% | | |

---

## Part 7: Interpret

- What share of national GDP comes from coastal tourism?
- How does this compare to total tourism's share of GDP?
- What policy implications might this have?

---
---

## Answer Key

### Part 1: Coastal Arrivals

- International coastal arrivals = 500,000 x 0.35 = **175,000**
- Domestic coastal trips = 2,000,000 x 0.20 = **400,000**
- Total coastal arrivals = 175,000 + 400,000 = **575,000**

### Part 2: Weighted Tourism Partial

- Total expenditure weight for international = 500,000 x 2.5 = **1,250,000**
- Total expenditure weight for domestic = 2,000,000 x 1.0 = **2,000,000**
- Total expenditure weight = 1,250,000 + 2,000,000 = **3,250,000**
- Coastal-weighted expenditure (international) = 1,250,000 x 0.35 = **437,500**
- Coastal-weighted expenditure (domestic) = 2,000,000 x 0.20 = **400,000**
- Total coastal-weighted expenditure = 437,500 + 400,000 = **837,500**
- **Weighted tourism partial = 837,500 / 3,250,000 = 25.8%**

### Part 3: Coastal Tourism Expenditure

- Coastal tourism expenditure = 800 x 0.258 = **USD 206.2 million**

### Part 4: Coastal Tourism GVA

- GVA-to-expenditure ratio = 320 / 800 = **0.40 (40%)**
- Coastal tourism GVA = 206.2 x 0.40 = **USD 82.5 million**

### Part 5: Coastal Tourism Employment

- Total arrivals = 500,000 + 2,000,000 = 2,500,000
- Coastal arrivals partial = 575,000 / 2,500,000 = **23.0%**
- Coastal tourism employment = 45,000 x 0.230 = **10,350 jobs**

### Part 6: Completed OTSA Summary Table

| Indicator | Total Tourism | Tourism Partial | Coastal/Ocean Tourism |
|-----------|--------------|----------------|-----------------------|
| Arrivals (number) | 2,500,000 | 23.0% | 575,000 |
| Expenditure (USD M) | 800 | 25.8% | 206.2 |
| Direct GVA (USD M) | 320 | 25.8% | 82.5 |
| Employment (jobs) | 45,000 | 23.0% | 10,350 |
| % of national GDP | 6.4% | -- | 1.6% |

### Part 7: Interpretation

- **Coastal tourism contributes approximately 1.6% of national GDP** (82.5 / 5,000)
- Total tourism contributes 6.4% of GDP, so coastal tourism represents about **25% of the total tourism economy**
- This means roughly one-quarter of the tourism sector is directly driven by coastal and marine assets
- **Policy implications:**
  - Coastal ecosystems (beaches, coral reefs, marine biodiversity) are significant economic assets that justify conservation investment
  - Degradation of coastal environments could put 1.6% of GDP and over 10,000 jobs at risk
  - There is a strong economic case for integrating marine conservation with tourism development planning
  - Monitoring coastal tourism through an OTSA provides an evidence base for sustainable tourism policies
