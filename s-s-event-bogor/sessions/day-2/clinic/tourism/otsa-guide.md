# Ocean Tourism Satellite Account -- Step-by-Step Guide

An OTSA is simpler than a full OESA because it focuses on one sector done in depth: tourism. This guide walks through each step.

---

## Step 1: Define the Coastal Tourism Boundary

The first step is deciding which tourism activities are "ocean-related." This requires defining both the **activity scope** and the **geographic scope**.

### Activities to include

- Beach visits and swimming
- Diving and snorkeling
- Surfing, kiteboarding, stand-up paddleboarding
- Fishing charters and recreational fishing
- Whale watching and marine wildlife tours
- Coastal hiking and nature walks
- Marine park and marine reserve visits
- Cruise tourism (port calls)
- Sailing and yachting

### Industries to include

- **Accommodation** in coastal areas (hotels, resorts, guesthouses within a defined distance of shore -- typically 5-10 km)
- **Food and beverage** in coastal areas (restaurants, bars, cafes serving tourists in coastal zones)
- **Recreation and entertainment** (dive operators, boat tour companies, marine park concessions)
- **Transport** (ferries, water taxis, coastal charter services)
- **Travel agencies and tour operators** offering marine/coastal packages

### Tips for defining the boundary

- Use a consistent geographic definition (e.g., "within X km of the coastline" or "in coastal municipalities")
- When in doubt, include an activity and adjust later via the partial -- it is better to be inclusive at this stage
- Document your boundary decisions clearly for transparency and replicability

---

## Step 2: Estimate the Tourism Partial

The tourism partial is the share of total national tourism that is coastal or marine. There are three main methods.

### Method 1: Visitor Motivation Survey

Ask tourists directly about their primary motivation. The tourism partial equals the share who report coastal/marine activities.

- **Data needed:** Visitor survey with motivation questions
- **Where to find it:** National tourism authority, exit surveys at airports/ports, accommodation guest surveys
- **Strengths:** Most directly measures tourist intent
- **Limitations:** Requires a well-designed survey; may not capture secondary motivations

### Method 2: Geographic Proxy

Use the share of tourism infrastructure or bed-nights in coastal areas as a proxy.

- **Data needed:** Accommodation statistics by location (coastal vs. inland)
- **Where to find it:** National statistics office, accommodation registers, tourism boards
- **Strengths:** Straightforward; uses readily available administrative data
- **Limitations:** Assumes coastal visitors are ocean-motivated (some may be there for other reasons)

### Method 3: Activity-Based

Estimate the share of total tourism expenditure that goes to marine/coastal activities and services.

- **Data needed:** Tourism expenditure breakdown by activity type
- **Where to find it:** Tourism satellite account, visitor expenditure surveys
- **Strengths:** Directly linked to spending
- **Limitations:** Requires detailed expenditure data, which may not be available

### Summary Table

| Method | Data Needed | Where to Find It | Best When... |
|--------|------------|-------------------|--------------|
| Visitor motivation survey | Survey with motivation questions | Tourism authority, exit surveys | You have or can conduct visitor surveys |
| Geographic proxy | Accommodation stats by location | Statistics office, accommodation registers | Detailed surveys are unavailable |
| Activity-based | Expenditure by activity type | TSA, expenditure surveys | A TSA already exists with activity breakdowns |

### Combining Methods

In practice, you may use a combination. For example, use the visitor survey for international tourists and the geographic proxy for domestic tourists. Document which method you used and why.

---

## Step 3: Compile Indicators

Apply the tourism partial to national-level tourism data to derive coastal/ocean tourism indicators.

### Calculations

1. **Coastal visitor arrivals** = Total arrivals x Tourism partial
2. **Coastal tourism expenditure** = Total tourism expenditure x Tourism partial
3. **Tourism direct GVA** = Coastal expenditure - Intermediate inputs (or derive from the TSA if available)
4. **Coastal tourism employment** = Total tourism employment x Coastal share

### Notes

- If the country has a TSA, you can use its GVA figures directly and apply the coastal partial
- Employment can be estimated using the same partial, or using a separate employment-specific partial if data are available
- Always note whether your figures include only direct effects, or also indirect and induced effects

### Interpreting the Results

- Compare coastal tourism GVA to national GDP to show its relative importance
- Compare to other ocean economy sectors (from the OESA) to show tourism's role within the ocean economy
- Track over time to monitor trends

## Data Source References

- **UNWTO:** International tourism statistics and Tourism Satellite Account methodology -- [https://www.unwto.org](https://www.unwto.org)
- **National tourism boards:** Arrivals data, accommodation statistics, visitor surveys
- **TSA Recommended Methodological Framework (2008):** Standard methodology for compiling tourism satellite accounts
- **GOAP Technical Guidance on OESA:** Methodology for integrating tourism into ocean economy accounts
- **SEEA-EA (2021):** Framework for ecosystem accounting, including cultural services (recreation) that complements the OTSA
