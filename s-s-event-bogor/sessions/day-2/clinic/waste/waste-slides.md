# Ocean Waste and Emissions Accounts -- Clinic Walkthrough Slides

**Time:** 15-30 min depending on participant level
**Purpose:** Guide a fellow through building physical flow accounts for waste, water emissions, and air emissions related to the marine environment

---

## Slide 1: What are waste/residuals accounts?

Waste and residuals accounts are **SEEA Central Framework physical flow accounts** that track what the economy puts into the marine environment. They sit on the economy side of the environment-economy boundary.

| Component | What it records |
|-----------|----------------|
| Water emissions | Substances discharged from the economy to water bodies and seas |
| Solid waste | Waste entering the marine environment from land-based and sea-based sources |
| Air emissions from ocean industries | Greenhouse gases and pollutants from maritime sectors |

These are different from SEEA Ecosystem Accounting. SEEA-CF tracks what goes **into** the environment. SEEA-EA tracks what happens to the ecosystem **as a result**.

---

## Slide 2: Why it matters for ocean management

Marine pollution is one of the most visible threats to ocean health. Waste and emissions accounts connect that pollution to its economic sources.

**Key policy links:**
- SDG 14.1: Reduce marine pollution of all kinds, in particular from land-based activities
- SDG 14.3: Minimize and address the impacts of ocean acidification
- Regional seas conventions (HELCOM, OSPAR, Noumea Convention)
- National pollution regulations, waste management frameworks, emission standards

**What these accounts answer:**
- Which industries generate the most marine pollution?
- How much waste reaches the ocean, and what type?
- Are emission reduction policies working for ocean industries?

---

## Slide 3: Three account types for the ocean context

| Account | What it tracks | Physical unit | Key sources |
|---------|---------------|---------------|-------------|
| Water emissions | Substances discharged to sea | tonnes/yr by substance | Industry, sewerage, agriculture runoff |
| Solid waste to sea | Waste entering the marine environment | tonnes/yr by type | Coastal municipalities, ships, fishing vessels |
| Air emissions (ocean industries) | GHG and pollutants from ocean sectors | tonnes CO2e/yr | Shipping, fishing fleets, offshore energy |

Each account follows the same logic: record **who generates** the residual (by industry and households) and **where it goes** (treatment, recycling, or direct discharge to the environment).

---

## Slide 4: Water emissions account structure

The water emissions account has a supply side and a use side.

**Supply side -- who generates the emissions:**
- Industries (by ISIC sector): food processing, chemicals, textiles, mining
- Households (domestic sewage)
- Agriculture (nutrient runoff from fertilizer and livestock)

**Use side -- where do they go:**
- Collected by sewerage system and treated (partially removed)
- Direct discharge to rivers, estuaries, and sea
- Leakage from septic systems and informal settlements

**Key substances to track:**
- Nitrogen (N) and phosphorus (P) -- cause eutrophication
- Biochemical oxygen demand (BOD) -- organic pollution load
- Heavy metals (mercury, cadmium, lead) -- toxic and persistent
- Chemicals (pesticides, industrial solvents)

---

## Slide 5: Solid waste account for the ocean

**Types of waste entering the marine environment:**
- Plastics: macroplastics (packaging, containers) and microplastics (fragments, fibres)
- Fishing gear: abandoned nets, traps, lines (ghost fishing)
- Ship waste: garbage, cargo residues, sewage
- Construction debris: coastal development waste
- Sewage sludge: disposed at sea
- Dredging material: harbour and channel maintenance

**Sources:**
- Land-based sources account for roughly 80% of marine debris
- Sea-based sources (shipping, fishing, offshore platforms) account for roughly 20%

**Key metric:** tonnes/yr entering the marine environment by waste type and source sector.

---

## Slide 6: Air emissions from ocean industries

Maritime shipping accounts for 2-3% of global greenhouse gas emissions. Other ocean industries also contribute.

**Ocean sectors with significant emissions:**
- Maritime shipping (international and domestic)
- Fishing vessel fuel combustion
- Offshore oil and gas operations
- Port operations (cargo handling, vessel movements in port)

**Account structure:**
- Take the ocean economy sectors identified in your OESA
- Apply emission factors (fuel consumption x emission factor per fuel type)
- Compile by sector and substance: CO2, CH4, N2O, SO2, NOx, PM

**Reference:** IMO Fourth GHG Study (2020) provides emission factors for the maritime sector.

---

## Slide 7: Two pathways

**Pathway A -- National statistics with coastal/ocean partials**
1. Start with national waste statistics, wastewater data, or GHG inventories
2. Identify the coastal or ocean-related share (similar to OESA partials)
3. Estimate the fraction that reaches the marine environment
4. Compile into account tables

**Pathway B -- Primary data from environmental monitoring**
1. Water quality monitoring stations (pollutant concentrations and loads)
2. Beach litter surveys (standardized item counts and weights)
3. Port waste reception facility records
4. Vessel fuel consumption records
5. Compile directly into account tables

Most countries will use a combination of both pathways, depending on what data exist for each sub-account.

---

## Slide 8: What to do right now

1. **Identify which waste and emissions data your country already collects.** Check with:
   - Environment ministry (water quality monitoring, pollution permits)
   - Port authority (ship waste records, port emissions)
   - Waste management agency (solid waste generation, collection rates)
   - Fisheries agency (fleet size, fuel use)
   - Statistics office (national GHG inventory)

2. **Pick the pollution type most relevant to your policy question.** You do not need to build all 3 sub-accounts at once.

3. **Start with the data you have.** A partial account using available data is more useful than a comprehensive account that never gets built.

**Need help?** Ask a facilitator to walk you through the step-by-step guide for your chosen sub-account.
