**Time:** 20-30 minutes

## Scenario

Your task: apply ocean economy partials to the national data and compile an OESA summary table.

## Part 7: Interpretation

1. **Coastal tourism** contributes the most to ocean GVA at USD 114.0 million (33.5% of total ocean GVA).

2. **Coastal tourism** employs the most people at 13,500 ocean jobs, followed by fisheries at 11,400. Together these 2 sectors account for 68% of ocean employment.

3. The ocean economy share of GDP is **17.0%**. This is typical for a SIDS. Small island developing states generally have ocean economy shares of 10-30%, reflecting the central role of marine resources in their economies.

4. **Coastal tourism** has the largest output and GVA, but also the largest workforce. Growth potential depends on the policy objective:
   - For GVA growth: tourism and construction have the largest absolute values
   - For employment: tourism and fisheries are the most labour-intensive
   - For diversification: marine minerals and transport are relatively small and could be developed

5. Policy questions the OESA can answer that national GDP alone cannot:
   - How dependent is the economy on the ocean? (17% of GDP)
   - Which ocean sectors drive growth and employment?
   - How would a decline in fisheries or tourism affect the national economy?
   - Where should the government invest to grow the ocean economy?
   - How does the ocean economy compare to other countries in the region?
