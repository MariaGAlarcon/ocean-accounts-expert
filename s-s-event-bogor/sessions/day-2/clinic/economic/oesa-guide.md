# Ocean Economy Satellite Accounts -- Step-by-Step Guide

## Structure

This guide provides two pathways for compiling an Ocean Economy Satellite Account:

- **Pathway A: Rapid assessment** -- use available industry statistics and administrative records to produce a first estimate. No Supply-Use Tables required.
- **Pathway B: Full SUT-based compilation** -- anchor the account to Supply-Use Tables for a balanced, official statistical product. Follows the 7-step methodology.

Start with Pathway A if you do not have access to SUTs. Move to Pathway B when SUTs become available.

---

# PATHWAY B: FULL SUT-BASED COMPILATION

Use this pathway when you have access to Supply-Use Tables. It follows the 7-step process and produces a balanced, internally consistent account.

## Step 1: Define scope

Set the boundaries for your OESA.

**Geographic scope:**

| Boundary | Definition | Source |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| Exclusive Economic Zone (EEZ) | Maritime territory extending 200 nautical miles from baseline | VLIZ Maritime Boundaries Geodatabase (marineregions.org) |
| Coastal zone | Land area within defined distance of coastline | National coastal zone legislation or 100 km default |
| National maritime territory | Territorial sea (12 nm) + contiguous zone (24 nm) + EEZ | UNCLOS definitions |

**Economic scope -- three pillars:**

| Pillar | Definition | Test |
|--------|-----------|------|
| On-water production | Activities on or under the ocean | Would this activity exist without the ocean surface or seabed? |
| Coastal-dependent | Activities that require a coastal location | Would this activity relocate inland if the coast did not exist? |
| For-ocean-use | Goods produced for ocean activities | Is this product designed for use on, in, or relating to the ocean? |

## Step 3: Select framework

Anchor the account to **Supply-Use Tables (SUTs)**.

SUTs provide gross output and intermediate consumption by industry in a balanced accounting framework. They are the standard base for satellite accounts in the SNA.

| What SUTs provide | How the OESA uses it |
|-------------------|---------------------|
| Gross output by industry | Multiply by ocean partial to get ocean output |
| Intermediate consumption by industry | Multiply by ocean partial to get ocean IC |
| Value added by industry | Cross-check: ocean GVA should equal ocean output minus ocean IC |
| Employment by industry (if included) | Multiply by ocean partial to get ocean employment |

### Where to get the data

| Data needed | Source | URL |
|-------------|--------|-----|
| Supply-Use Tables | National Statistical Office | Country-specific |
| UN National Accounts Statistics | UN Statistics Division | unstats.un.org/unsd/snaama |
| SUT compilation guidance | UN Handbook of Supply-Use Tables | unstats.un.org |
| OECD SUTs (if member country) | OECD STAN database | oecd.org |

If SUTs are not available for your country, check whether the NSO publishes GDP by industry (production approach). This provides GVA directly, though without the supply-use balance.

## Step 4: Estimate ocean partials

For each mapped industry, determine the share attributable to the ocean economy.

**Partial estimation methods:**

| Method | Description | Best for |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Direct classification | Industry is 100% ocean by definition | Fishing (0311), water transport (5011, 5012) |
| Business register | Count ocean establishments as share of total | Ship building, marine equipment |
| Geographic proxy | Share of industry activity in coastal municipalities | Tourism, construction |
| Administrative records | Use agency data (port tonnage, fishing licenses) | Transport, fisheries |
| Revenue share | Ocean-related revenue as share of total industry revenue | Mining (offshore vs onshore) |
| Expert judgement | Informed estimate where data are sparse | Last resort only |

## Step 6: Data integration and consistency

Check that your account is internally consistent and does not double count.

| Check | What to verify | Action if failed |
|-------|---------------|-----------------|
| SUT reconciliation | Sum of ocean industry outputs equals total ocean output | Recheck partials and industry mapping |
| No double counting | Each establishment is counted in only 1 sector | Review classification of multi-activity firms |
| Partial consistency | Partials for related industries are logically consistent | If tourism partial is 0.3, food service partial in tourist areas should be similar |
| GVA identity | Ocean GVA = Ocean output - Ocean IC for every industry | Recalculate; check for rounding errors |
| Employment plausibility | Ocean employment share is plausible relative to GVA share | Labour-intensive sectors (fishing, tourism) will have higher employment share than GVA share |

## Step 7: Validate and interpret

Compare your results to benchmarks and document all assumptions.

**Validation checks:**

| Benchmark | Expected range | Source |
|-----------|---------------|--------|
| Ocean economy as share of GDP | 1-10% for most countries | OECD (2016), country OESAs |
| SIDS ocean economy share | 5-30% | Belize OESA (10%), Seychelles (30%+) |
| Fisheries share of ocean GVA | 5-30% for developing countries | FAO State of World Fisheries |
| Tourism share of ocean GVA | 10-40% for coastal/island nations | UNWTO tourism satellite accounts |

**Documentation checklist:**

- [ ] Reference year and currency
- [ ] Geographic scope (EEZ, coastal zone definition)
- [ ] List of industries included and excluded, with rationale
- [ ] Partial for each industry, with method and data source
- [ ] RAG rating for each partial
- [ ] Known data gaps and limitations
- [ ] Comparison to at least 1 other country OESA
