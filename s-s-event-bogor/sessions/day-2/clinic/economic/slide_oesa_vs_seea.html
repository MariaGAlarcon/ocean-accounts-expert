OESA vs SEEA-EA: Two Sides of Ocean Accounts

Complementary frameworks for understanding the ocean economy and ocean ecosystems
⚗

Ocean Economy (OESA)

Based on
System of National Accounts (SNA)
Measures
Market-based economic activity
Key Indicator
Gross Value Added (GVA), GDP contribution
Sectors
Fisheries, minerals, transport, tourism, construction, ship building
Data From
National statistical offices, Supply-Use Tables
Output
Ocean GDP as % of national GDP
“How big is the ocean economy?”
☘

Ecosystem Accounts (SEEA-EA)

Based on
System of Environmental-Economic Accounting
Measures
Ecosystem health and non-market values
Key Indicator
Condition Index (0–1), ecosystem service values
Accounts
Extent, Condition, Ecosystem Services
Data From
Remote sensing, field surveys, ecological monitoring
Output
Condition trends, service valuations
“How healthy is the ocean and what does it provide?”

Together they give the full picture: OESA shows what the ocean contributes to the economy. SEEA-EA shows what the economy depends on from the ocean. One without the other is incomplete.
