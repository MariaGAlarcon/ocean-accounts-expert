Ocean Accounts: One Integrated Framework
All components share the same spatial foundation and connect to each other

These are not separate alternatives to SEEA-EA. They are all essential components of the same Ocean Accounting framework, connected through Basic Spatial Units.

⚖

Ocean Economy Satellite Account (OESA)

How big is the ocean economy? Reorganize national accounts to measure ocean GDP.

When: You want to measure fisheries, shipping, tourism, mining contribution to GDP

☼

Ocean Tourism Satellite Account (OTSA)

What does coastal tourism contribute? A focused satellite account for ocean-dependent tourism.

When: Tourism is a major sector and you need economic data

☢

Waste & Emissions (SEEA-CF)

What pollution enters the ocean? Track water emissions, solid waste, and air emissions from ocean industries.

When: You want to measure pollution, plastics, or shipping emissions

♥

Social & Governance (GOAP)

Who benefits? Who is affected? Compile data on wellbeing, livelihoods, equity, culture.

When: You want to understand the human dimension of ocean management

Ocean Accounts is one unified framework. Environment (SEEA-EA), economy (OESA), pollution (SEEA-CF), and society (GOAP) are interconnected components -- compiled once to a shared spatial framework, serving many policy needs.
