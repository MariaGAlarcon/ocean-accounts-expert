# Condition Account Exercise

**Time:** 20-30 minutes
**What you need:** Calculator or Excel, this sheet
**Based on:** Belize coral reef condition accounts (simplified)

## Scenario

You have coral reef monitoring data from 4 sites in a marine reserve. Surveys were done in 2019 (opening) and 2024 (closing). Three condition indicators were measured.

### Raw data -- Opening (2019)

| Site | Live coral cover (%) | Fleshy macroalgae cover (%) | Fish biomass (kg/ha) |
|------|---:|---:|---:|
| Site A | 28.5 | 22.0 | 180 |
| Site B | 35.2 | 18.5 | 245 |
| Site C | 18.7 | 38.0 | 120 |
| Site D | 42.1 | 12.0 | 310 |

### Raw data -- Closing (2024)

| Site | Live coral cover (%) | Fleshy macroalgae cover (%) | Fish biomass (kg/ha) |
|------|---:|---:|---:|
| Site A | 22.0 | 28.5 | 160 |
| Site B | 30.8 | 24.0 | 220 |
| Site C | 15.2 | 42.0 | 105 |
| Site D | 38.5 | 15.0 | 290 |

### Reference levels

| Indicator | Reference level | Direction | Source | SEEA EA method |
|-----------|----------------|-----------|--------|---------------|
| Live coral cover | 50% | Higher is better | Regional Indo-Pacific benchmark (1970s Caribbean baselines) | 3/5: Statistical / contemporary |
| Fleshy macroalgae cover | 60% | Higher is worse | Maximum degraded state (prescribed ecological threshold) | 6: Prescribed level |
| Fish biomass | 500 kg/ha | Higher is better | McClanahan et al. 2011 (unfished reef biomass) | 1: Reference sites |

Note: These are illustrative reference levels for the exercise. For your own account, select reference levels appropriate to your region using one of the 6 SEEA EA methods (see the condition guide, Step 2). The key requirement is to document which method you used and why.

## Answer Key

### Part 1 -- Opening (2019)

| Site | Coral CI | Macroalgae CI | Fish CI |
|------|---:|---:|---:|
| A | 0.57 | 0.63 | 0.36 |
| B | 0.70 | 0.69 | 0.49 |
| C | 0.37 | 0.37 | 0.24 |
| D | 0.84 | 0.80 | 0.62 |

### Part 1 -- Closing (2024)

| Site | Coral CI | Macroalgae CI | Fish CI |
|------|---:|---:|---:|
| A | 0.44 | 0.53 | 0.32 |
| B | 0.62 | 0.60 | 0.44 |
| C | 0.30 | 0.30 | 0.21 |
| D | 0.77 | 0.75 | 0.58 |

### Part 2 -- Means

Opening: Coral 0.62, Macroalgae 0.62, Fish 0.43
Closing: Coral 0.53, Macroalgae 0.55, Fish 0.39

### Part 3 -- Condition Account

| Indicator | Reference | Opening CI | Closing CI | Change | Direction |
|-----------|----------|--------:|--------:|--------:|-----------|
| Live coral cover | 50% | 0.62 | 0.53 | -0.09 | Declining |
| Macroalgae cover | 60% | 0.62 | 0.55 | -0.07 | Declining |
| Fish biomass | 500 kg/ha | 0.43 | 0.39 | -0.04 | Declining |

### Part 4

1. Coral cover declined most (-0.09). Possible drivers: bleaching events, storm damage, disease, sedimentation from coastal development.
2. Macroalgae CI is declining (0.62 to 0.55), meaning macroalgae cover is increasing. This is ecologically consistent with coral decline: as coral dies, macroalgae colonizes the available substrate.
3. Site C is the most degraded across all indicators. Recommend investigating local stressors (sedimentation, overfishing, pollution sources) and considering targeted management interventions such as fishing restrictions or water quality improvement.
4. Composite opening = (0.62 + 0.62 + 0.43) / 3 = 0.56. Composite closing = (0.53 + 0.55 + 0.39) / 3 = 0.49. The reserve is declining overall (-0.07).
