# Ocean Accounts Clinic

## 1\. Purpose

The Ocean Accounts Clinic provides **structured consultation time** between country partners and CSDR-GOAP technical staff across Days 2 and 3 of the Exchange. It runs in two parts:

* **Part 1 — Hands-On Account Creation (Day 2).** Direct support compiling, structuring, or troubleshooting ocean accounts using participant data. Advisors work with country teams on account design, data harmonisation, and quality assurance through the AI platform.  
* **Part 2 — Policy Implications & Brief Development (Day 3).** Strategic consultation on how ocean accounts connect to policy processes, financing instruments, and decision-making frameworks — including blue bonds, GEF proposals, NDC/NBSAP integration, MPA management effectiveness, climate finance, and blue economy strategies. Includes hands-on policy brief development.

Before your consultation, please complete the [**GOAP Metadata Sheet**](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1JeXF8jyG3WSpz95IqrkpGUxSFFPYP74H/edit?usp=sharing&ouid=100724715549401459501&rtpof=true&sd=true).

## 4\. How to Maximise Your Clinic Time

Clinic sessions are short. The following preparation steps will ensure advisors can spend time solving problems rather than orienting:

1. **Complete the [GOAP Metadata Sheet](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1JeXF8jyG3WSpz95IqrkpGUxSFFPYP74H/edit?usp=sharing&ouid=100724715549401459501&rtpof=true&sd=true) before your session.** This is the single most important preparation step. Sessions where metadata has not been documented will need to spend significant time on this rather than account compilation or policy linkages.  
2. **Bring your data ready to use.** Have datasets loaded on your laptop in their working format (shapefile, CSV, Excel, raster). If files are large or stored on government servers, download them in advance — do not rely on conference Wi-Fi for large transfers.  
3. **Know your data sharing permissions.** Confirm in advance whether your institution allows data to be shared with GOAP for processing, and whether any restrictions apply. If government clearance is required, obtain it before the event. This avoids reaching a dead end mid-session.  
4. **Prepare a clear objective.** Come with a specific question or goal — e.g., "I need help structuring a condition account for our coral reef monitoring data" or "I want to understand how our mangrove extent accounts can feed into our NDC update."  
5. **Bring relevant policy documents.** For Part 2 (policy) sessions, bring copies of the national policy, strategy, or financing proposal you want to connect accounts to (NDC, NBSAP, blue economy strategy, GEF concept note, bond framework, etc.).

## 5\. Materials & Logistics

**Room Setup:** Ballroom 1-2, IPB Convention Center. Table seating for small-group consultations, power outlets, projector/screen for screen-sharing, Wi-Fi access.

**Advisor Kit:** Printed intake forms, sample dashboards and policy briefs for demonstration, data quality checklist, reference sheets for financial instruments (blue bond term sheets, GEF project criteria, GCF eligibility), and session log sheets for MEL tracking.

## 6\. Monitoring, Evaluation & Follow-up

After each session, the advisor completes a brief session log capturing: participant country, clinic part attended, primary outcome (e.g., account structure agreed, finance pathway identified, data submitted to AI platform), follow-up actions and responsible party, and whether the participant was connected to a specific CoP or peer partner.

Post-event, GOAP Secretariat sends each clinic participant a follow-up email within 2 weeks containing any referenced resources and a timeline for next steps.
