How the methods connect

Carbon (Example 1)
Uses extent account (area) multiplied by literature rate, then valued using Social Cost of Carbon. Most accessible method -- needs only extent data and a published NCP rate.
Fish (Example 2)
Uses catch statistics and market data. Valued via resource rent (revenue minus intermediate costs). Requires economic data on fishing costs.
Coastal Protection (Example 3)
Uses extent account (reef length) and replacement cost of built infrastructure. Annualized to produce annual service flow.

Key Insight: Regulating and cultural services dominate

Recreation (46%) and coastal protection (43%) together account for 89% of the total annual value. These non-market services are invisible without ocean accounts. Provisioning (fish) contributes only 7% of total value, yet it typically receives the most policy attention. Ocean accounts reveal the hidden economic importance of reef and mangrove conservation for tourism revenue and disaster risk reduction -- a powerful argument for Marine Spatial Planning and blue finance.
