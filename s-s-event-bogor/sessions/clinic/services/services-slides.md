# Ecosystem Services Accounts -- Clinic Walkthrough Slides

**Time:** 15-30 min depending on participant level
**Purpose:** Guide a fellow through building an ecosystem services account

---

## Slide 1: What is a services account?

An ecosystem services account quantifies the **benefits that ecosystems provide to people**, in both physical and monetary terms.

| Part | What it measures | Unit examples |
|------|-----------------|--------------|
| Physical supply | How much service the ecosystem provides | kg fish/yr, Mg CO2/yr, visitor trips/yr |
| Monetary value | Economic significance of that service | USD/yr (exchange values, not welfare values) |

Services accounts depend on extent and condition accounts: you need to know where ecosystems are (extent) and how healthy they are (condition) to estimate what services they provide.

## Slide 2: Types of ecosystem services

| Category | Service | Ecosystem | Physical measure |
|----------|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Provisioning** | Wild fish catch | Coral reefs, seagrass, pelagic | kg/yr |
| | Wood / fuel | Mangroves | tonnes/yr |
| **Regulating** | Carbon sequestration | Mangroves, seagrass | Mg CO2/yr |
| | Coastal protection | Coral reefs, mangroves | Coastline length (m) or buildings protected |
| | Nursery habitat | Coral reefs, seagrass | Juvenile fish biomass (kg/yr) |
| | Sediment retention | Coral reefs | m3 CaCO3/yr |
| **Cultural** | Recreation / tourism | Coral reefs, mangroves | Visitor trips/yr, expenditure |
| | Gleaning / subsistence | Seagrass, intertidal | Hours/yr, harvest kg/yr |

**Start with 1-2 services most relevant to your policy question.** You do not need to value all services.

---

## Slide 3: Two pathways

**Pathway A -- No primary data (Tier 1, value transfer)**

Use published values from similar ecosystems:
1. Identify which services are relevant to your policy question
2. Find comparable valuation studies (ESVD database, literature)
3. Transfer values to your context (adjust for income, area, ecosystem type)
4. Report with clear caveats about transfer uncertainty

**Pathway B -- You have data (Tier 2, primary calculation)**

Calculate from your local data:
1. Compile physical data (catch records, visitor statistics, carbon stock estimates)
2. Calculate physical supply per ecosystem type
3. Apply valuation method (resource rent, replacement cost, expenditure analysis)
4. Build the supply and use table

---

## Slide 4: Valuation methods at a glance

| Method | When to use | Example |
|--------|------------|---------|
| **Resource rent** | Marketed goods (fish, timber) | Fish revenue minus costs of labour, fuel, gear, capital |
| **Replacement cost** | Regulating services with no market | Cost to build a seawall if coral reef coastal protection were lost |
| **Social cost of carbon** | Carbon sequestration | Mg CO2/yr x SCC (e.g., USD 51/tonne CO2) |
| **Direct expenditure** | Tourism and recreation | Visitor spending attributable to the ecosystem |
| **Equivalent wage** | Unpaid/subsistence activities | Hours of gleaning x local wage rate |
| **Value transfer** | No local data available | Published USD/ha/yr from similar ecosystem, adjusted |

## Slide 6: Quick calculations you can do now

**Carbon sequestration (if you have extent data):**
```
Annual sequestration = Ecosystem extent (ha) x Net carbon production rate (Mg CO2/ha/yr)
Annual value = Sequestration x Social Cost of Carbon (USD/Mg CO2)
```

Literature rates: Mangroves ~3.7 Mg CO2/ha/yr, Seagrass ~1.3 Mg CO2/ha/yr

Example: 500 ha mangroves x 3.7 Mg CO2/ha/yr = 1,850 Mg CO2/yr
Value: 1,850 x USD 51 = USD 94,350/yr

**Fish provisioning (if you have catch data):**
```
Resource rent = Total catch revenue - (Labour + Fuel + Gear + Capital costs)
```

**Tourism (if you have visitor numbers):**
```
Reef recreation value = Reef visitors/yr x Average reef-attributable spending per visitor
```

## Slide 7: What to do right now

**If you have no data:**
1. Pick 1-2 services relevant to your policy question
2. Search the Ecosystem Services Valuation Database (ESVD) for your ecosystem type and region
3. Calculate a Tier 1 estimate using value transfer (published USD/ha/yr x your extent)
4. Document the source study and how comparable it is to your context

**If you have data:**
1. Pick the service with the best available data
2. Calculate physical supply
3. Apply the appropriate valuation method
4. Fill in the supply table

**Need help choosing a method?** Ask a facilitator.
