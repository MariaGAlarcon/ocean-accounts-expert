## Scenario

### Available data

**Fisheries:**
- Total reef-associated fish catch: 180,000 kg/yr
- Average market price: USD 3.50/kg
- Total fishing costs (labour, fuel, gear, capital): USD 420,000/yr

**Carbon:**
- Mangrove NCP rate: 3.7 Mg CO2/ha/yr (from literature)
- Seagrass NCP rate: 1.3 Mg CO2/ha/yr (from literature)
- Social cost of carbon: USD 51/Mg CO2

**Tourism:**
- 15,000 reef visitors per year (divers and snorkelers)
- Average reef-attributable spending: USD 85/visitor

**Coastal protection:**
- 12 km of coastline fronted by coral reefs
- Replacement cost for equivalent seawall: USD 5,000/m
- Seawall lifespan: 50 years

## Part 6: Interpret

1. Which service has the highest monetary value? Does this match your intuition about what is most important for coastal communities?

2. Carbon sequestration uses the Social Cost of Carbon (USD 51/Mg CO2). If you used the voluntary carbon market price instead (e.g., USD 15/Mg CO2), how would the value change? Recalculate.

3. The coastal protection value uses replacement cost. What are the limitations of this method? Does building a seawall truly replace all the services a coral reef provides?

4. Fish provisioning uses resource rent. If fishing costs were higher (e.g., USD 600,000 instead of USD 420,000), what would happen to the resource rent? What does a negative resource rent mean?

## Answer Key

### Part 1
- Gross revenue: 180,000 x 3.50 = USD 630,000
- Resource rent: 630,000 - 420,000 = **USD 210,000/yr**

### Part 2
- Mangrove physical: 350 x 3.7 = **1,295 Mg CO2/yr**
- Mangrove monetary: 1,295 x 51 = **USD 66,045/yr**
- Seagrass physical: 800 x 1.3 = **1,040 Mg CO2/yr**
- Seagrass monetary: 1,040 x 51 = **USD 53,040/yr**
- Total carbon: 2,335 Mg CO2/yr, **USD 119,085/yr**

### Part 3
- Recreation: 15,000 x 85 = **USD 1,275,000/yr**

### Part 4
- Total replacement: 12,000 x 5,000 = USD 60,000,000
- Annualized: 60,000,000 / 50 = **USD 1,200,000/yr**

### Part 5 -- Monetary supply

| Service | Value (USD/yr) |
|---------|---:|
| Fish provisioning | 210,000 |
| Carbon sequestration | 119,085 |
| Recreation | 1,275,000 |
| Coastal protection | 1,200,000 |
| **Total** | **2,804,085** |

### Part 6

1. Recreation (USD 1.28M) and coastal protection (USD 1.2M) are the highest. This often surprises people who assume fisheries dominate. The result reflects the high cost of replacing natural coastal defense infrastructure.

2. At USD 15/Mg CO2: 2,335 x 15 = USD 35,025/yr (vs. USD 119,085 at SCC). The price used dramatically affects the value. SCC reflects global damages from carbon emissions; market price reflects what buyers currently pay. Both are legitimate for different purposes.

3. Replacement cost captures only the wave attenuation function. A seawall does not provide fish habitat, biodiversity support, carbon storage, tourism value, or sediment production. Replacement cost is a lower bound on the total value of the reef's regulating services.

4. Resource rent at USD 600,000 costs: 630,000 - 600,000 = USD 30,000. If costs exceeded revenue, resource rent = 0 (capped). The ecosystem still provides fish, but the economic activity is unprofitable. This does not mean the fish have no value to people (e.g., subsistence value), only that the commercial fishery does not generate economic surplus.
