# Policy Brief Clinic -- Facilitation Guide

**Session:** Ocean Accounting Clinic Pt.2 -- Policy Implications and Brief Development
**Day:** 3 (Wednesday, 1 April 2026)
**Time:** 09:10-10:30 and 10:45-12:00 (2.5 hours total, with coffee break)
**Location:** Meeting Room, IPB International Convention Center, Bogor
**Facilitator:** Maria Alarcon
**Participants:** Ocean Accounts fellows (early-career researchers, ~15-20 participants)

## Session goals

By the end of this session, every participant should have:

1. A completed COMPASS Message Box that distils their ocean account results into a clear message for a specific audience
2. A first draft of a 2-4 page policy brief connecting their account findings to a policy question
3. Written peer feedback on their draft from a fellow participant

## Session flow

### Part 1: Message Box and Concepts (09:10-10:30)

| Time | Duration | Activity | What to do |
|------|----------|----------|------------|
| 09:10 | 5 min | Welcome and session goals | Show Slides 1-2. Frame the session: "You are here to turn your Day 2 account work into something a decision-maker can use. You will leave with two things: a Message Box and a draft policy brief." |
| 09:15 | 10 min | The science-policy gap and audience | Show Slides 3-4. Ask the room: "Who here has written something for a non-scientist audience before?" Let 2-3 people share briefly. Transition: "The first step is understanding that policymakers read information in the opposite order from scientists." |
| 09:25 | 10 min | Introduce the COMPASS Message Box | Show Slides 5-6. Walk through the five sections. Use the GBF worked example. Pass around the printed GBF brief so participants can see how the Message Box maps to a real document. |
| 09:35 | 20 min | Individual Message Box exercise | Show Slide 7, then let participants work. Circulate the room. The most common sticking point is the "So What" -- fellows tend to describe their methods instead of why the audience should care. Prompt: "If a minister read only one sentence from your box, which would it be?" Also watch for Issues that are too broad ("ocean conservation") -- help narrow them ("tracking reef health for MPA management in Samoa"). |
| 09:55 | 10 min | Pair share and refine | Ask participants to swap Message Boxes with their neighbor. The reviewer checks: (1) Can I understand the So What without knowing their technical background? (2) Is the Issue in one short phrase? After 7 minutes, ask 1-2 pairs to share what they improved. |
| 10:05 | 10 min | Policy brief anatomy and writing tips | Show Slides 8-10. Walk through the GOAP brief structure using one of the printed examples. Hold up the DRR brief and point to each section: "This is the key messages box -- notice it fits in half a page. This is the context -- it starts with the reader's world, not the author's methods." |
| 10:15 | 10 min | Real GOAP examples and range of topics | Show Slide 11. Invite participants to browse the printed briefs on the central table. Point out: "These all draw on ocean accounts, but they speak to completely different audiences: a finance ministry, a DRR agency, a CBD focal point. Your brief will find its own angle." |
| 10:25 | 5 min | Preview Part 2 | Show Slide 12. Distribute the policy brief template if not already at seats. Explain: "After the break, you will draft your brief section by section. Start with context, write key messages last. The exercise worksheet has sentence starters for each section." |

**Coffee break 10:30-10:45**

During the break: check that all participants have the template and exercise worksheet. Replace any that were misplaced.

### Part 2: Drafting and Review (10:45-12:00)

| Time | Duration | Activity | What to do |
|------|----------|----------|------------|
| 10:45 | 5 min | Recap and drafting instructions | Brief recap of the structure. Remind: "Use your Message Box as source material. Start with context, write key messages last. The account-type adaptation table on your exercise worksheet can help if you are unsure how your account connects to policy." |
| 10:50 | 10 min | Write title and context section | Circulate. The most common problem is a vague title. Prompt: "Can your reader tell the country and topic from the title alone? If not, make it more specific." Check that context sections start with the reader's world, not "Ocean accounts are a framework that..." |
| 11:00 | 15 min | Write "what the accounts show" and policy implications | Circulate. Help fellows select their single most striking finding. Many will try to include all their Day 2 results -- encourage them to pick one headline number and one supporting point. Prompt: "What is the single most striking finding from your account?" |
| 11:15 | 10 min | Write recommendations | Circulate. Check that recommendations are specific and actionable. Red flags: "More research is needed" (not actionable), "Countries should adopt ocean accounts" (too broad). Better: "Integrate mangrove extent data into the next NBSAP revision by 2027" or "Establish annual reef condition monitoring at 5 priority sites within the MPA network." |
| 11:25 | 5 min | Write key messages box | Remind: "Pull 3-4 bullets from what you already wrote. Each should stand on its own. No jargon. A busy reader should get the point in 30 seconds." |
| 11:30 | 15 min | Peer review using checklist | Ask participants to swap drafts with their neighbor (ideally someone different from the Part 1 pair). Use the 8-item peer review checklist on the exercise worksheet. Both partners review simultaneously. After 12 minutes, ask reviewers to write one strength and one suggestion on the sheet before returning it. |
| 11:45 | 10 min | Volunteers share key messages | Ask 2-3 volunteers to read their key messages aloud (not the whole brief, just the bullets). After each, ask the room: "Could you tell the country, the topic, and what should be done?" Celebrate progress. |
| 11:55 | 5 min | Next steps and closing | Remind fellows: (1) This is a first draft, not a final product. (2) They can continue refining after the event. (3) The GOAP Secretariat can provide feedback on drafts -- share the contact email. (4) The printed GOAP briefs are examples of the finished product they are working toward. Thank participants. |

## Backup plan: fellows without completed Day 2 accounts

Some fellows may not have finished an account on Day 2. Two options:

**Option A: Reverse-engineer a Message Box from a real brief.** Give them one of the printed GOAP briefs and ask them to fill in a blank Message Box based on the brief's content. This teaches the same skills (audience identification, message distillation) and produces a usable artifact. They can then start a brief template using their own country context and the policy angle from the example.

**Option B: Use the GBF brief as a starting point.** Ask the fellow to draft a country-specific version of the GBF brief, adapting the recommendations and context to their own country's biodiversity framework. They know their country context even without completed account data.

## Account-type adaptation table

Use this if fellows are unsure how their account type connects to policy. This table is also included in the exercise worksheet.

| Account type | Policy angle to suggest | Example "So What" to prompt with |
|-------------|------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Extent | Habitat loss tracking for NBSAP/GBF targets | "Mangrove loss of X ha between 2015 and 2023 means Y km of coastline lost natural storm protection" |
| Condition | Ecosystem health trends for MPA effectiveness | "Coral cover in managed areas declined at the same rate as unprotected reefs, raising questions about current MPA management" |
| Services | Valuing ecosystem contributions to livelihoods and GDP | "Coastal ecosystems provide an estimated $X million in storm protection services annually, but this value is declining" |
| OESA | Ocean economy baseline for blue economy strategy | "Ocean industries contribute X% of GDP but monitoring systems cannot track whether growth is sustainable" |
| OTSA | Tourism sustainability and ecosystem dependency | "Coastal tourism employs X people and generates $Y, but depends on ecosystem health that is declining" |
| Waste | Pollution sources and trends for regulation | "Plastic waste entering coastal waters increased X% over 5 years, exceeding safe thresholds in Y% of monitored sites" |
| Social | Governance gaps and community participation | "Only X% of coastal communities participate in MPA planning despite depending on marine resources for their livelihoods" |

## Troubleshooting common issues during the session

| Issue | What to do |
|-------|-----------|
| Fellow's Message Box Issue is too broad ("ocean sustainability") | Ask: "What specific part of this can your ocean account measure?" Help narrow to a concrete, measurable topic. |
| Fellow writes methods in the So What section | Redirect: "That tells me what you did. The So What tells your reader why they should care about the result. What changes for them if they read your finding?" |
| Fellow cannot identify a target audience | Suggest: "Think about who funded your data collection, or who asked for the information. If no one asked yet, who would benefit most from knowing your results?" |
| Fellow's recommendations are too vague | Apply the specificity test: Does it name who should act? Does it name what they should do? Does it connect to a real decision or deadline? |
| Fellow wants to include everything from Day 2 | Remind: "A policy brief is not a report. Pick your single strongest finding. If the reader wants more detail, they will ask -- and that is the conversation you want to start." |
| Fellow is stuck on an empty page | Point them to the sentence starters in the exercise worksheet. Sometimes the hardest part is the first sentence. The starters give them something to edit rather than something to create from scratch. |
| English as a second language -- fellow struggles with writing | Encourage them to write in their own language first if that helps, then translate the key messages. The thinking matters more than the polish. They can refine the English later. |
