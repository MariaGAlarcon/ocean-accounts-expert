# Discussion Questions for Teerapong's Session

**Session:** How to get started and diagnosis tool?
**Lead:** Teerapong (UNESCAP), supported by GOAP Technical Team
**Time:** 10:25 to 11:00 (35 min)
**Your role:** Support, ask questions to draw out key points and encourage fellow participation

---

## Questions to prompt during the SEEA-EA Framework Overview (~10 min)

After Teerapong explains the Extent, Condition, Services, Benefits sequence:

1. "Teerapong, for fellows who are just starting, do they need to do all four account types, or can they start with just one? What determines where to begin?"

2. "Can you give an example of how the tiered approach works in practice? If a country has satellite data but no field surveys, what tier are they at and what can they still produce?"

3. "How does this framework connect to what countries are already reporting for SDG 14? Is there overlap, or are these separate exercises?"

## Questions to prompt during Ocean Accounts Architecture (~15 min)

After Teerapong covers extent, condition, services:

4. "For the fellows working on coral reefs or mangroves, what is the minimum data needed to produce a basic extent account? Can global datasets like Allen Coral Atlas or Global Mangrove Watch get them started?"

5. "Condition accounts require reference levels. How do countries set these when they do not have historical baselines?"

6. "For ecosystem services, some fellows may work in contexts where market values do not capture the full picture. How does the framework handle non-market services like coastal protection or cultural value?"

## Questions to prompt during Policy Applications (~10 min)

After Teerapong covers SDGs, NDCs, NBSAPs:

7. "Can you show fellows how a completed ocean account actually feeds into an NDC update? What does that look like in practice?"

8. "For fellows from SIDS, the ocean is the economy. How do ocean accounts help make the case for climate finance or blue bond proposals?"

## Questions to prompt from fellows (encourage audience participation)

If the room is quiet, use these to break the ice:

9. "Who here has worked with SEEA before in any form, even terrestrial? Raise your hand." [Then:] "What was the hardest part?"

10. "Who has data on their laptop right now that they want to turn into an account this week?" [Then:] "Tell us in one sentence what that data is."

11. "What is the one policy question your government needs an ocean account to answer?"

---

## Tips for facilitating

- Stand near the fellows, not next to Teerapong. You are there to represent the audience perspective.
- If Teerapong gets technical, jump in with: "Can you give a concrete example of what that looks like for a country just starting out?"
- Watch for confused faces. If you see them, ask Teerapong to pause and check understanding.
- Use the questions above as prompts, not a script. Pick the ones that fit the flow of the conversation.
- If a fellow asks a great question, amplify it: "That is an important point, does anyone else face the same challenge?"
