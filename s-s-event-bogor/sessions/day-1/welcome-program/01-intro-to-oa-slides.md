## Slide 2: Why are we here?

**The ocean supports economies, livelihoods, and ecosystems across the Global South. But we manage it with incomplete information.**

Three questions ocean accounts answer:

1. What economic output comes from the ocean?
2. What benefits do people and communities get from the ocean, and how are these distributed?
3. Is ocean development sustainable over time?

*Speaker notes: These three questions come directly from the GOAP Technical Guidance. Frame the problem: governments make decisions about fisheries, MPAs, blue carbon, coastal development, but lack integrated data connecting the environment, economy, and society. Ocean accounts fill that gap.*

---

## Slide 3: What are ocean accounts?

**A structured framework for compiling and integrating data about marine and coastal environments and their relationship with human societies and economies.**

Three things that make ocean accounts different from other ocean data:

- Organized according to the Ocean Accounts Framework (consistent, comparable, coherent)
- Compatible with international statistical standards (SNA, SEEA)
- Fit for policy decision-making (not just research)

*Speaker notes: Ocean accounts are not a data collection exercise. They are an information system that transforms diverse data into standardized, decision-relevant outputs. They sit alongside national economic accounts, not separate from them.*

## Slide 4: The building blocks

**What goes into an ocean account?**

[Diagram showing the framework components]

| Component | What it measures | Example |
|-----------|-----------------|---------|
| Extent accounts | Area of ecosystems | Mangrove cover in km2, change over time |
| Condition accounts | Ecosystem health | Coral reef condition index (0 to 1 scale) |
| Ecosystem services | Benefits to people | Fish provisioning, carbon sequestration, coastal protection |
| Ocean economy | Economic activity | Value added by fisheries, tourism, shipping |
| Social accounts | Community wellbeing | Livelihoods, food security, distributional equity |

*Speaker notes: Walk through the table briefly. Emphasize that countries do not need to do all of these at once. Most start with one or two components based on policy priorities and available data.*

## Slide 7: The rapid account generation process

**How we get from your data to a dashboard**

1. You bring your data (shapefile, CSV, Excel, raster)
2. We work together to structure it into an account framework
3. The AI platform processes it into standardized outputs
4. You get a dashboard and the basis for a policy brief
