# Speed Networking & Partnership Mapping -- Facilitation Guide

**Session:** 11:00 to 12:00 (60 min)
**Lead:** Indonesia OA Fellows (facilitation), Maria (overall coordination)
**Audience:** ~32 Ocean Accounts Fellows
**Location:** Meeting Room 2

---

## Materials to prepare

### Print before the event

- [ ] **Fellow profile cards** (32 copies) -- A5 card stock, one per fellow. Pre-printed with fields to fill in by hand. See template below.
- [ ] **Thematic station signs** (5 signs) -- A3 or A4 landscape, bold text, printed on colored paper or card. One per station.
- [ ] **"Partnership bingo" cards** (32 copies) -- A5 or half-A4, one per fellow. See template below.
- [ ] **Post-it notes** -- 3 colors (e.g., green, yellow, pink), ~50 of each color
- [ ] **Markers** -- thick tip, 1 per fellow (or shared per table)
- [ ] **Chart paper or flip chart sheets** (5) -- one per thematic station, pre-labeled with station name
- [ ] **Bell or timer** -- for signaling rotations (phone timer works)
- [ ] **Music playlist** (optional) -- upbeat background music during networking rounds, mute during instructions

### Room setup

- Clear the center of the room so fellows can form two parallel lines
- Push chairs/tables to the sides
- Set up 5 thematic stations around the room perimeter (table or flip chart stand each)
- Have a projector/screen available for instructions

---

## ROUND 1: Speed Introductions (11:00 to 11:15, 15 min)

### Format: Two Lines Facing Each Other

**Setup (2 min):**
- Ask everyone to stand and form two lines of equal length, facing each other
- Each person should be directly facing a partner
- If odd number, Maria or an Indonesia fellow joins to even it out

**Instructions to give:**
> "You have 3 minutes with the person in front of you. Use your profile card as a guide. Share: your name, country, what you work on, and what data you brought or deal with. When you hear the bell, the line on my right shifts one step to the right. The person at the end wraps around to the other end. You will meet 4 different people."

**Rotation schedule:**
| Round | Time | Duration |
|-------|------|----------|
| Pair 1 | 11:02-11:05 | 3 min |
| Pair 2 | 11:05-11:08 | 3 min |
| Pair 3 | 11:08-11:11 | 3 min |
| Pair 4 | 11:11-11:14 | 3 min |

**Bell script:**
- "Introduce yourselves -- go!" [start timer]
- [3 min] BELL. "Thank you! Line on my right, shift one step to the right. New partner -- go!"
- Repeat 3 more times

**At the end (1 min):**
> "You have just met 4 colleagues from across the world. Keep your profile card visible throughout the day. Now, hold on to your partnership bingo card -- you will need it for the next round."

---

## ROUND 2: Partnership Bingo (11:15 to 11:35, 20 min)

### Format: Free-roaming Bingo Challenge

This replaces the static thematic clustering with an active, game-style exercise that gets people moving and talking with purpose.

**Instructions to give:**
> "You each have a bingo card with 9 squares. Each square describes a type of person or expertise. Your job is to find a fellow who matches each square, have a short conversation with them, and write their name in the square. You need to fill at least one full line (3 in a row, column, or diagonal) to complete the challenge. You have 15 minutes. Go!"

**Rules:**
- Each square must be a different person (no repeats)
- You must actually talk to the person (not just ask "are you this?")
- When you complete a line, come to the front and show Maria

**First 3 fellows to complete a line get recognized** (small prize if available, or just applause and bragging rights).

At the 10-minute mark, announce: "5 minutes left! If you have not started, find anyone and start talking."

At 15 minutes: BELL. "Stop! Who has a line?"

**Debrief (5 min, 11:30-11:35):**
> "Raise your hand if you found someone working on the same ecosystem type as you."
> "Raise your hand if you found someone from a different region with a similar policy challenge."
> "Who found the most surprising connection?"

Pick 2-3 fellows to share a 30-second answer.

---

## ROUND 3: Thematic Partnership Mapping (11:35 to 11:55, 20 min)

### Format: Station Rotation with Post-it Contributions

**5 thematic stations around the room:**

| Station | Color | Sign text |
|---------|-------|-----------|
| 1 | Blue | Marine Spatial Planning & MPAs |
| 2 | Green | Blue Carbon & Climate |
| 3 | Orange | Fisheries & Ocean Economy |
| 4 | Purple | Ecosystem Condition & Biodiversity |
| 5 | Red | Social Accounts & Community Engagement |

**Instructions to give:**
> "Go to the station that is closest to your work. If you are between two topics, pick the one where you want to find partners. You have 8 minutes at your station."

**At each station, fellows do 3 things (guided by the chart paper prompts):**

The chart paper at each station has three sections pre-drawn:

```
STATION: [Theme Name]
-----------------------------------------
| WHO IS HERE?           | Write your   |
| (name + country)       | name and     |
|                        | country on   |
|                        | a GREEN      |
|                        | post-it      |
-----------------------------------------
| WHAT DO WE NEED?       | Write one    |
| (shared challenges,    | challenge or |
| data gaps, method      | need on a    |
| questions)             | YELLOW       |
|                        | post-it      |
-----------------------------------------
| WHAT CAN WE SHARE?     | Write one    |
| (data, methods, tools, | thing you    |
| contacts, experience)  | can offer    |
|                        | on a PINK    |
|                        | post-it      |
-----------------------------------------
```

**After 8 minutes:** BELL.
> "Look at your station's board. In 2 minutes, pick the most interesting match between a need and an offer. One person from each station will share it."

**Station reports (5 min, 11:47-11:52):**
One fellow per station stands and shares in 1 minute:
- "At the [theme] station, we have [X] people from [countries]. The biggest shared challenge is [Y]. And [person] from [country] has [Z] to offer."

**Wrap-up (11:52-11:55):**
> "These charts stay up all day. Add more post-its as you think of things. By the end of the Exchange, we want to turn these into concrete collaboration plans."

---

## CLOSING (11:55 to 12:00, 5 min)

**Maria wraps up:**

> "In the last hour you have met colleagues from [X] countries, discovered shared challenges, and started mapping what you can do together. This afternoon in the Partnership Summit you will hear the strategic side of these same themes. Tonight, continue these conversations over dinner."

**Action items for fellows:**
1. Complete the GOAP Metadata Sheet before tomorrow's Clinic
2. Prepare your datasets on your laptop
3. Keep your profile card and bingo card for the rest of the event
4. Add to the thematic station charts whenever you have new ideas

**Transition:** "Lunch is now. We reconvene in the plenary at 13:00."
