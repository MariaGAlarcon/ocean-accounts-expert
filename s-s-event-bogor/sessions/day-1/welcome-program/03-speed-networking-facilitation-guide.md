# Speed Networking & Partnership Mapping -- Facilitation Guide

**Session:** 11:00 to 12:00 (60 min)
**Lead:** Indonesia OA Fellows (facilitation), Maria (overall coordination)
**Audience:** ~32 Ocean Accounts Fellows
**Location:** Meeting Room 2

---

## Materials needed

- [ ] 5 thematic station signs (printed, see `printing/` folder)
- [ ] 5 flip chart sheets or large paper, one per station, pre-drawn with 3 sections (see station template below)
- [ ] Post-it notes, 3 colors (green, yellow, pink), ~30 of each
- [ ] Markers, enough for fellows to share at each station
- [ ] Your slide deck on the projector (slides 9 and 10 stay up as reference during the activity)

## Room setup

- Clear center space so fellows can form two parallel lines
- Set up 5 thematic stations around the room perimeter (table or flip chart stand with the pre-drawn chart paper)
- Post-its and markers already at each station

---

## ROUND 1: Speed Introductions (11:00 to 11:30, 30 min)

**Put slide 9 on screen.**

### Setup (2 min)

Ask everyone to stand and form two lines of equal length, facing each other. Each person should be directly facing a partner. If odd number, you or an Indonesia fellow joins to even it out.

### Instructions

> "You have 3 minutes with the person in front of you. The slide on screen shows what to share: your name, country, organization, ocean accounting focus, what data you work with, and one thing you want to learn this week. When I say 'next,' the line closest to the door shifts one step to the right. The person at the end wraps around. You will meet several different people."

### Run the rotations

Do as many rotations as time allows (aim for 7-8 pairs in 28 minutes):

- Call "Go!" to start each round
- After 3 minutes, call "Next! Line by the door, shift one to the right."
- Repeat

Keep it moving. If conversations are flowing well, you can stretch to 3.5 minutes. If energy dips, shorten to 2.5.

### Transition (2 min, ~11:28)

> "Thank you! You have just met colleagues from across the world. Now we are going to find out who shares your thematic interests."

---

## ROUND 2: Thematic Partnership Mapping (11:30 to 11:55, 25 min)

**Switch to slide 10 on screen.**

### 5 thematic stations

| Station | Sign text |
|---------|-----------|
| 1 | Marine Spatial Planning & MPAs |
| 2 | Blue Carbon & Climate |
| 3 | Fisheries & Ocean Economy |
| 4 | Ecosystem Condition & Biodiversity |
| 5 | Social Accounts & Community Engagement |

### Instructions (1 min)

> "Go to the station closest to your work. If you are between two topics, pick the one where you most want to find collaborators. You have 10 minutes."

### At each station (10 min)

The chart paper has three sections. Fellows use post-its:

- **GREEN post-it:** Write your name and country, stick it in the "Who is here?" section
- **YELLOW post-it:** Write one shared challenge or data gap you face
- **PINK post-it:** Write one thing you can offer (data, methods, tools, experience, contacts)

Walk around the stations. If a group is quiet, prompt them:
- "What is the biggest data gap holding back your work?"
- "Does anyone have experience with [relevant topic] they can share?"
- "Are any of you working on the same ecosystem or coastline? Could you share data?"

### Station synthesis (2 min)

> "Look at your board. Pick the most interesting match between something someone needs and something someone else can offer."

### Station reports (10 min)

One fellow per station stands and shares in ~1.5 minutes:
- Who is at this station (countries)
- The biggest shared challenge
- The most promising collaboration opportunity

After each report, ask the room: "Anyone from another station who connects to this? Raise your hand."
