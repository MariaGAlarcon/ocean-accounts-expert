# Distributional Supply-Use Table by BSU and Household Group

**Accounting area:** Tuvana (national)
**Accounting period:** 2021-22

This is the core innovation: the ecosystem services supply-use table disaggregated by both BSU (spatial) and household income group (distributional). It answers: "How much of each service from each ecosystem reaches each household group?"

**Key finding:** The Q1:Q5 ratio flips across BSUs. In the urban BSU, richer households get 2.8x more fish (regressive, like Australia). In the outer island BSU, the poorest households get 2.5x more fish (strongly progressive). The subsistence pathway is the mechanism: where subsistence is dominant, the poorest benefit most because they fish the most.

## Accounting identity checks

### Fish provisioning: supply = use across all BSUs

| BSU | Supply (t) | Household use (t) | Non-HH use (t) | Total use (t) | Balance |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| BSU-1 | 25 | 17 | 8 | 25 | 0 |
| BSU-2 | 505 | 302 | 203 | 505 | 0 |
| BSU-6 | 460 | 455 | 5 | 460 | 0 |
| **National** | **6,421** | **3,820** | **2,601** | **6,421** | **0** |

**Identity holds at every BSU.** Total supply = total use. No double counting.

### Cross-BSU flows (fish traded between BSUs)

| From BSU | To BSU | Tonnes | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|---|
| BSU-2 (Viti North) | BSU-1 (Nasova Urban) | 120 | Market sale |
| BSU-5 (Lau North) | BSU-1 (Nasova Urban) | 35 | Market sale + gifts to urban relatives |
| BSU-6 (Lau Central) | BSU-1 (Nasova Urban) | 28 | Market sale + gifts |
| BSU-8 (Offshore) | BSU-1 (Nasova Urban) | 180 | Tuna processing plant |
| BSU-8 (Offshore) | Export | 3,800 | Export market |

**These cross-BSU flows are critical.** Urban households receive fish from multiple BSUs. The supply-use identity must account for these transfers: fish produced in BSU-2 but consumed in BSU-1 is recorded as supply in BSU-2 and use in BSU-1. The national total still balances.

### Double counting check: subsistence fish

| Where fish appears | Table | Amount | Edge |
|---|---|---|---|
| Supply-use table (physical product) | Table U1 | 1,428 tonnes | E9 (product) |
| Social flows table (activity hours) | See Table 03 | 142,800 hours | E10 (activity) |
| Social stocks table (food security) | See Table 04 | Fish consumption per capita | E5/E6 (outcome) |

**No double counting.** The fish (tonnes) appears once. The fishing activity (hours) appears once. The food security outcome (kg/capita) is derived from the supply-use table, not added separately. The three-table system (supply-use, social flows, social stocks) is mutually consistent without overlap.
