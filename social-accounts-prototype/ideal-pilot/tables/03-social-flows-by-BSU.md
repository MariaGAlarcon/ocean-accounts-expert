### Spatial patterns

**Subsistence fishing hours per household:**
- BSU-1 (urban): 0.08 hrs/HH/yr (negligible)
- BSU-2 (Viti North): 8.9 hrs/HH/yr
- BSU-6 (Lau Central): 31.7 hrs/HH/yr
- Outer islands average: 32 hrs/HH/yr -- 400x the urban rate

**Gender patterns:**
- Subsistence fishing: 81% male, 19% female (national)
- Gleaning: 71% female, 29% male (national)
- Unpaid household labour enabling fishing: 85% female
- Combined: women provide ~42% of all subsistence-related social activity hours but receive less than 20% of the income from commercial fishing

## Table F3: Ecosystem-linked social activity change account

| Activity | BSU | Opening (2020) | Additions | Reductions | Closing (2022) | Driver |

| Coastal monitoring (hrs) | BSU-5 | 1,800 | +600 (new MPA designation) | 0 | 2,400 | Governance change (E8) |

**The change account links ecosystem condition to social activity.** In BSU-6, seagrass decline (CI 0.68 to 0.62) reduced gleaning productivity, resulting in 1,850 fewer gleaning hours as some gleaners shifted to other activities. This is the social consequence of ecosystem degradation -- quantified in the accounting framework.
