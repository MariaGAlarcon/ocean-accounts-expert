# Social Stocks Tables: Opening/Closing Accounts for Social Conditions

These tables parallel the SEEA-EA condition account format: they record the state of social conditions at two points in time (opening and closing) and track changes. The accounting identity is:

**Opening Stock + Net Changes = Closing Stock**

For countable indicators (MPA coverage, agreements, licences), this identity holds exactly. For index-based indicators (trust, life satisfaction), the identity describes measured change but the "additions" and "reductions" may not be decomposable in the same way as ecosystem extent.

### Accounting identity for human capital

For additive indicators (fish consumption in kg, count of people):
- Opening stock (e.g., 42 kg/capita/yr) + Net change (e.g., -3 kg) = Closing stock (39 kg/capita/yr)

For rate/proportion indicators (% in poverty, % stunted):
- Opening rate + Change in rate = Closing rate
- But population denominators may change, so track both numerator and denominator

For index indicators (life satisfaction, ocean connectedness):
- Opening score + Change = Closing score
- Not decomposable into "additions" and "reductions" in the same way as extent
- Report as measured change with confidence intervals

## Condition-style normalization: Should social stocks have a condition index?

In SEEA-EA condition accounts, biophysical indicators are normalized to a 0-to-1 condition index (CI) using reference levels. Could social stocks use the same approach?

### Where normalization works

| Indicator | Reference level | Normalization | CI formula |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fish consumption | WHO recommended 20 kg/yr | Higher is better up to reference | CI = min(measured/reference, 1.0) |
| Stunting prevalence | WHO "low" threshold 20% | Lower is better | CI = 1 - (measured/max_acceptable) |
| MPA coverage | Kunming-Montreal target 30% | Higher is better up to target | CI = min(measured/target, 1.0) |
| Compliance rate | 100% compliance | Higher is better | CI = measured/100 |

### Where normalization is problematic

| Indicator | Problem |
|---|---|
| Life satisfaction | No universal reference level; culture-dependent |
| Trust index | What constitutes "full trust"? No ecological equivalent of pristine reference |
| Cooperation index | Same problem -- no undisturbed baseline |
| Ocean connectedness | Inherently subjective and culturally variable |

### Recommendation

Use condition-style normalization only for indicators with defensible, externally-defined reference levels (WHO thresholds, SDG targets, legal requirements). For subjective and index-based indicators, report raw values and changes without normalization. This is an honest approach -- do not force a condition index where no meaningful reference exists.

## Connecting social stocks to ecosystem stocks

The power of these tables comes from reading them alongside ecosystem accounts:

| Ecosystem account | Social stock account | Connection question |
|---|---|---|
| Coral reef extent: -164 ha (decline) | Fish consumption Q1: ? change | Did reef decline affect poorest households' protein access? |
| Coral condition index: 0.36 to 0.44 (improving) | Tourism employment: ? change | Did reef recovery translate to more tourism jobs? |
| Mangrove extent: +0.2 ha (stable) | Coastal protection beneficiaries: ? change | Are the same vulnerable households still protected? |
| Seagrass condition: declining | Gleaning hours: ? change | Are gleaners losing access to productive beds? |

This cross-reading is what makes social accounts part of an integrated system rather than a standalone social dashboard. The next file (04) builds the flow tables that track the activities connecting these stocks.

## Open questions

1. **Aggregation across capital types:** Can human capital, social capital, and institutional capital be aggregated into a single "social condition index" analogous to an ecosystem condition index? The SEEA-EA aggregates multiple biophysical indicators into a composite CI. Is this meaningful for social conditions, or do the dimensions remain incommensurable?

2. **Reference levels for social stocks:** Ecosystem condition uses ecological reference levels (pristine, undisturbed). What is the social equivalent? SDG targets provide some reference points, but they are aspirational rather than biophysical baselines.

3. **Spatial resolution:** Ecosystem accounts use BSUs for spatial disaggregation. Social stocks should map to the same BSUs where possible, but household survey data is often available only at larger administrative units. How to reconcile?

4. **Frequency:** Ecosystem condition surveys may run annually or every 2 to 5 years. Social surveys (census, DHS, labour force) have different frequencies. Can the accounting period for social stocks align with ecosystem accounting periods?
