# Natural Capital Accounts of the Maldives (Laamu Atoll)

This repository contains the methodology report and supporting materials for compiling the first Natural Capital Accounts (NCA) of the Maldives, with Laamu Atoll as the pilot case study.

## Overview

This project presents Natural Capital Accounts aligned with the UN System of Environmental-Economic Accounting (SEEA) framework. The accounts provide a comprehensive assessment of the extent, condition, and economic value of key marine and coastal ecosystems in Laamu Atoll, specifically:

- **Coral reefs** — approximately 6,692 hectares
- **Seagrass meadows** — approximately 4,220 hectares
- **Mangroves** — approximately 4.8 hectares

## Key Findings

- Wild fish provisioning from reef ecosystems supported an estimated annual catch of **28.6 metric tons**, supporting local food security.
- Carbon sequestration by mangroves and seagrass meadows captured approximately **3,484.7 metric tons of carbon** in 2020, valued at 10.66 million rufiyaa.
- Coastal protection services benefited buildings across residential and tourism islands by protecting **33 km of coastline**, with an annual valuation of 82.85 million rufiyaa.
- Approximately **44.3%** of all tourism spend on Laamu could be related to coral reefs.
